> "**Compliance Overview** — the big number at the top. This is the percentage

> "**Overdue Training** — members who are already past their certification
> expiration dates. Red flags that need immediate attention."

> "**Recent Completions** — training that's been recently recorded, either by you
> or by members submitting their own external training."

## CHAPTER 3: Training Programs & Requirements (4:00 – 8:00)

### UNDERSTANDING PROGRAMS VS. REQUIREMENTS (4:00 – 5:00)

> "Before we create anything, let's understand the hierarchy. **Training
> Programs** are collections of requirements. Think of a program as a
> curriculum — 'Firefighter I Certification' or 'Annual Recertification.'"

> "**Training Requirements** are the individual items within a program — 'CPR
> Certification,' 'Hazmat Awareness,' 'Ladder Operations.' Each requirement has
> a recurrence interval — how often it needs to be renewed."

**[CALLOUT: Two-level diagram: "Program" → contains → "Requirements"]**

### CREATING A TRAINING PROGRAM (5:00 – 6:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Training Programs (TrainingProgramsPage). Click "Create
Program."]**

> "Let's create an annual recertification program. Click 'Create Program.'"

**[SCREEN: Fill in the program form]**

> "**Program Name:** 'Annual Firefighter Recertification.'
> **Description:** 'Required annual training and certification renewals for all
> active firefighters.'
> **Target Audience:** Select which positions or membership types this program
> applies to — in this case, all active members."

**[SCREEN: Select target positions/groups]**

> "**Deadline:** You can set a program-level deadline or let individual
> requirement deadlines govern."

### ADDING REQUIREMENTS (6:30 – 7:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Training Requirements (TrainingRequirementsPage)]**

> "Now let's add the individual requirements. Click 'Add Requirement.'"

**[SCREEN: Fill in a requirement form]**

> "**Requirement Name:** 'CPR/AED Certification.'
> **Category:** 'Medical' — categories help organize and filter.
> **Recurrence:** 'Every 2 years' — the system will automatically flag members
> when their completion date is approaching the two-year mark.
> **Required Hours:** If there's a minimum hour requirement, enter it here."

> "Create requirements for each item: CPR, Hazmat Awareness, SCBA Fit Testing,
> Blood-Borne Pathogens, Ladder Operations — whatever your department and state
> require."

**[SCREEN: Show several requirements created in a list]**

### LINKING REQUIREMENTS TO PROGRAMS (7:30 – 8:00)

> "Once your requirements exist, add them to your program. Open the program,
> click 'Add Requirements,' and select the ones that belong."

**[SCREEN: Show adding requirements to a program]**

> "Now when a member views this program, they see every requirement with their
> completion status — green checks for completed, warnings for expiring, red X's
> for missing."

**[TRANSITION: Recording training]**

## CHAPTER 4: Recording Training Completions (8:00 – 12:00)

### RECORDING FOR INDIVIDUAL MEMBERS (8:00 – 9:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to a member's training history
(MemberTrainingHistoryPage)]**

> "There are several ways to record training completions. The most direct: go
> to a member's profile, click on their training history, and add a completion."

**[SCREEN: Click "Record Training" or "Add Completion"]**

> "Select the requirement — 'CPR/AED Certification.' Enter the completion date,
> the expiration date if applicable, any notes, and optionally upload a copy of
> the certification document."

**[SCREEN: Fill in the completion form with a sample cert]**

> "Having the cert document attached is a best practice — it's your proof of
> completion for audits and compliance checks."

### BULK RECORDING AFTER A CLASS (9:30 – 11:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Training Admin (TrainingAdminPage)]**

> "After running a training session — say you just did a department-wide CPR
> class — you don't want to enter completions one member at a time. Use the
> bulk recording feature."

**[SCREEN: Show bulk training recording interface]**

> "Select the training requirement — 'CPR/AED.' Set the completion date and
> expiration date. Then select all the members who attended — you can select
> individually or use 'Select All' for the whole roster."

**[SCREEN: Select multiple members, then submit]**

> "One click and everyone's records are updated. This is the fastest way to
> process training after a class."

### MEMBER SELF-SUBMISSION (11:00 – 12:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Submit Training page (SubmitTrainingPage) — show from
a member's perspective]**

> "Members can also submit their own external training. If someone takes a CPR
> class through the Red Cross on their own time, they can submit it through the
> platform."

**[SCREEN: Show the member submission form]**

> "The member selects the requirement, enters the details, and uploads their
> certificate. The submission goes to you for review and approval."

> "You'll see pending submissions in your dashboard. Review the documentation,
> approve or reject with notes, and the member's record is updated."

**[CALLOUT: "Member submissions require Training Officer approval"]**

## CHAPTER 5: Skills Testing (12:00 – 16:00)

### WHAT IS SKILLS TESTING? (12:00 – 13:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Skills Testing (SkillsTestingPage)]**

> "Skills testing goes beyond certifications — it's hands-on evaluation of a
> member's practical skills. Can they throw a ladder? Can they perform a search
> pattern? Can they operate the pump?"

> "The Logbook lets you create skills testing templates, conduct tests, and
> record results — all digitally."

### CREATING SKILL TEMPLATES (13:00 – 14:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Skills Testing Templates
(SkillsTestingTemplatesTab). Click to create a new template.]**

> "First, create a template. The Skill Template Builder lets you design a
> testing protocol."

**[SCREEN: Navigate to the Skill Template Builder
(SkillTemplateBuilderPage)]**

> "**Template Name:** 'Annual SCBA Proficiency Test.'
> **Skills to Evaluate:** Add each skill being tested — 'Don and doff SCBA in
> under 60 seconds,' 'Navigate a zero-visibility environment,' 'Emergency air
> management.'"

**[SCREEN: Add several skills to the template with pass/fail criteria]**

> "For each skill, set the evaluation criteria — pass/fail, scored 1-5, or
> timed. You can add detailed instructions for the evaluator and the member."

### CONDUCTING A SKILLS TEST (14:30 – 15:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Start Skill Test (StartSkillTestPage)]**

> "When it's time to test, select the template and the member being evaluated.
> The system presents each skill in order."

**[SCREEN: Show the active skill test interface (ActiveSkillTestPage)]**

> "Walk through each skill — mark it as pass or fail, add notes on performance,
> record the time if it's a timed evolution."

**[SCREEN: Show marking skills and adding notes]**

> "When the test is complete, the results are saved to the member's training
> record. You can see a history of all their skills test results over time."

### TEST RECORDS (15:30 – 16:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Skills Testing Test Records
(SkillsTestingTestRecordsTab)]**

> "The Test Records tab gives you a complete history of all skills testing
> conducted. Filter by member, by template, by date range, or by result."

**[SCREEN: Show filtering and browsing test records]**

> "This is your documentation for NFPA compliance — proof that your members are
> tested and proficient."

## CHAPTER 7: Integrating Training with Events & Wrap-Up (19:00 – 22:00)

### TRAINING EVENTS (19:00 – 20:00)

> "Training and Events are connected. When you create an event with the type
> 'Training,' you can link it to specific training requirements."

**[SCREEN: Show creating an event of type "Training" and linking a requirement]**

> "After the event, attendance records can feed directly into training
> completions. Members who checked in can be bulk-credited for the training
> requirement attached to that event."

> "This means: create the training event, members RSVP and check in, and after
> the event you batch-update everyone's training records with one action."

**[CALLOUT: "Event attendance → automatic training credit"]**

### EXTERNAL TRAINING (20:00 – 20:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to External Training page (ExternalTrainingPage)]**

> "The External Training page tracks training taken outside the department —
> courses at the fire academy, conferences, mutual aid training. Members submit
> these for your approval, and they count toward their compliance requirements
> just like internal training."

### TRAINING OFFICER'S WORKFLOW (20:30 – 21:30)

> "Let me summarize the Training Officer's workflow."

**[CALLOUT: Workflow steps appearing one at a time]**

> "**Weekly:** Check the dashboard for new submissions and approaching
> expirations. Approve or reject any member-submitted external training."

> "**Before Training Events:** Ensure the event is linked to the correct
> requirement. Prepare any skills testing templates needed."

> "**After Training Events:** Bulk-record completions for all attendees. Update
> skills testing records if practical evaluations were conducted."

> "**Monthly:** Review the Compliance Matrix. Identify members falling behind.
> Plan next month's training calendar to address gaps."

> "**Quarterly:** Generate compliance reports for the Chief and officers.
> Review and update training requirements if state mandates have changed."

### WRAP-UP (21:30 – 22:00)

> "The Training Officer role is about keeping the department ready. The Logbook
> automates the tracking, the reminders, and the reporting — so you can focus
> on the actual training."

> "Next up, we have the everyday member's guide — what the platform looks like
> for the Firefighter who just needs to RSVP, check their training status, and
> view the schedule."

**[SCREEN: End card with subscribe, next video link, and playlist link]**
