# Script 10: Your Training Pipeline — A Member's Guide

**Video Type:** Feature Deep Dive (Short/Medium-Form)
**Estimated Length:** 8–12 minutes
**Target Audience:** Recruits, probationary members, and any member enrolled in a training program
**Roles Covered:** firefighter, member (baseline), recruit
**Chapters:** 6 (each designed as a standalone clip)

> **Companion video:** Script 9 shows the officer's side of the same feature.
> This one is entirely from your seat as a member.

---

## CHAPTER 1: What's a Pipeline, and Why Are You In One? (0:00 – 1:30)

### HOOK (0:00 – 0:30)

**[SCREEN: A recruit opening their phone to the member dashboard, tapping a
"Recruit School — 62%" progress card.]**

> "If you're a new recruit, or working toward a certification, your department
> probably put you in a **training pipeline**. Think of it as the map for your
> journey — every step from where you are now to fully qualified, in one place,
> updating as you go. Let me show you how to read it and how to stay on track."

### THE BIG PICTURE (0:30 – 1:30)

> "A pipeline is an ordered path — usually broken into **phases**. You finish the
> requirements in one phase, you move to the next. Along the way you're earning
> hours, passing skills, completing courses. The best part? You mostly don't have
> to *do* anything to update it — it fills in from the training you're already
> doing. But knowing how to check it keeps you ahead."

## CHAPTER 2: Finding Your Progress — "You Are Here" (1:30 – 3:30)

### THE DASHBOARD WIDGET (1:30 – 2:15)

**[SCREEN: The member dashboard showing the training widget with up to three
active programs, each with a progress bar and "next steps".]**

> "Start on your dashboard. The training widget shows your active programs — up
> to three — each with a progress bar, your next steps, and any upcoming
> deadline. It's your at-a-glance 'am I okay?' check. Tap through for the full
> picture."

### THE FULL PROGRESS VIEW (2:15 – 3:30)

**[SCREEN: Training → My Training → "Active Program Enrollments" → click "View
full progress". Show the phase timeline with a "You are here" marker.]**

> "Go to **Training**, then **My Training**, find your program under **Active
> Program Enrollments**, and click **View full progress**. Here's your whole
> journey: your **current phase**, marked **'You are here'**, your overall
> percentage, your time remaining, your next milestones, and every requirement
> grouped by phase — with what's done and what's left."

**[CALLOUT: "'You are here' = your current phase"]**

> "This is the screen to check before you ask anyone 'what do I still need?' —
> it's all right there."

**[TRANSITION: What updates automatically]**

## CHAPTER 3: What Updates Your Progress Automatically (3:30 – 5:00)

### THE AUTOMATIC STUFF (3:30 – 4:30)

**[B-ROLL: A recruit at a live drill, then checking in at a shift, then a skills
evaluation — cut to the progress bar moving.]**

> "Most of your progress fills itself in. When an officer **approves a training
> session** you attended, the hours land on your requirement. When a shift
> officer **files a report** on your shift, your shift, call, and hour
> requirements move. When you **pass a skills test** that's tied to a
> requirement, that requirement completes. And a **course you took externally**
> can count too, once it's imported."

**[CALLOUT: "Sessions · Shifts · Skills tests · Imported courses — all automatic"]**

### WHY YOUR BAR MIGHT NOT MOVE YET (4:30 – 5:00)

> "One thing to know: some of these need an **officer to approve** first — a
> session has to be approved, a self-report has to be reviewed. So if you did the
> training but your bar hasn't moved, it may just be waiting on that approval.
> Give it a little time before you worry."

**[TRANSITION: Self-reporting]**

## CHAPTER 4: Logging Training You Did on Your Own (5:00 – 6:45)

### SUBMITTING EXTERNAL TRAINING (5:00 – 6:00)

**[SCREEN: Training → Submit Training. Fill in a course, upload a certificate,
submit.]**

> "Took a class on your own — a CPR refresher, a course at the academy? Log it.
> Go to **Submit Training**, pick the requirement it fulfills, enter the details,
> and — most important — **upload your certificate**. That's the proof your
> training officer needs. Then submit."

**[CALLOUT: "Always attach your certificate — it speeds up approval"]**

### WHAT HAPPENS NEXT (6:00 – 6:45)

> "Your submission goes to a training officer for review. You'll get a
> notification when it's approved — or if they need more from you. Here's the key
> thing to understand: **you submit, an officer approves.** You can't check off
> your own requirement to a hundred percent, and that's on purpose — it keeps
> everyone's record trustworthy. Your job is to log it and attach the proof; the
> credit comes when it's signed off."

**[TRANSITION: Staying on pace]**

## CHAPTER 5: Staying on Pace (6:45 – 8:30)

### THE PIPELINE WATCHES FOR YOU (6:45 – 7:45)

**[SCREEN: A notification: "You're falling behind in Recruit School — reach out
to a training officer if you need help catching up."]**

> "You've got a life outside the firehouse, and it's easy to drift behind without
> noticing. So the system keeps an eye out. If you're falling behind pace or a
> deadline is coming up and you're not where you should be, you'll get a
> heads-up — and so will your training officers. It's not a gotcha; it's an early
> nudge so you can catch up while there's still time."

### READING A DEADLINE (7:45 – 8:30)

**[SCREEN: The progress view highlighting "time remaining" and a milestone.]**

> "Your progress view always shows your **time remaining** and your next
> milestone. If a deadline's tight, that's your cue to log any outstanding
> training, ask about an upcoming session, or reach out to an officer. The
> earlier you flag it, the easier it is to fix."

**[TRANSITION: Advancing and leaving]**

---

## CHAPTER 6: Moving Forward — and Wrapping Up (8:30 – 10:00)

### ADVANCING PHASES (8:30 – 9:15)

**[SCREEN: The phase timeline advancing the "You are here" marker to the next
phase.]**

> "When you finish everything required in your current phase, you move to the
> next one automatically — and your 'You are here' marker jumps forward. Some
> phases, like a safety or certification phase, need an **officer to sign off**
> before you advance. If you're done but haven't moved, that's why — your officer
> gives the final nod."

> "One more thing: if you RSVP or check into a session for a phase you *haven't*
> reached yet, you'll see a friendly warning that it's for a later phase. You can
> still attend — just know it's ahead of where you are."

### LEAVING A PROGRAM (9:15 – 9:45)

**[SCREEN: The progression view → "Leave program".]**

> "And if a program no longer applies to you — say you stepped back from a level
> you don't need to maintain — you can **Leave program** from your progression
> view. It drops off your active dashboard and stops the reminders. Your history
> is kept, and an officer can re-enroll you later if things change."

### WRAP-UP (9:45 – 10:00)

> "That's your pipeline: check it on your dashboard, let the automatic feeds do
> most of the work, submit anything you did on your own, and act on the nudges
> when they come. Stay on the map, and certified takes care of itself."

**[SCREEN: End card with subscribe and playlist link]**
