# Script 6: The Member Experience — Your Day-to-Day Guide

**Video Type:** Role-Based Guide (Medium-Form)
**Estimated Length:** 15–20 minutes
**Target Audience:** All members — Firefighters, Engineers, Lieutenants, anyone using the platform daily
**Roles Covered:** firefighter, engineer, lieutenant, member (baseline)
**Chapters:** 7 (each designed as a standalone clip)

---

## CHAPTER 1: Introduction — Your Platform, Your Way (0:00 – 1:30)

### HOOK (0:00 – 0:30)

**[SCREEN: Quick montage from a member's perspective — checking an event on
a phone, scanning a QR code at the firehouse, viewing a shift schedule on a
tablet, checking training status on a laptop.]**

> "No more group texts asking who's coming to drill. No more paper sign-in
> sheets. No more wondering if your CPR cert is still valid. The Logbook handles
> all of it, and in this video, I'll show you exactly how to use it — as a
> member."

### WHAT YOU CAN DO (0:30 – 1:30)

> "As a member, you have view access to most of the platform. You can see
> events and RSVP, view the schedule and request shift swaps, check your
> training and certification status, browse documents and SOPs, view the member
> directory, vote in elections, and manage your own profile."

**[CALLOUT: Member capabilities list]**

> "You can't change other people's data or configure system settings — that's
> for officers and admins. But everything related to *your* participation in
> the department is right at your fingertips."

> "Let's start with logging in."

**[TRANSITION: Login walkthrough]**

## CHAPTER 2: Logging In & Your Dashboard (1:30 – 4:00)

### FIRST LOGIN (1:30 – 2:30)

**[SCREEN: Open browser to The Logbook login page (LoginPage)]**

> "You'll get your login credentials from your IT Manager or whoever set up the
> system. Open your browser — this works on your computer, tablet, or phone —
> and go to your department's Logbook URL."

**[SCREEN: Enter email and password. Click Login.]**

> "Enter your email and password, and click Login. If your department requires
> two-factor authentication, you'll be prompted for your authenticator code
> after entering your password."

**[SCREEN: Show 2FA prompt if applicable]**

> "If this is your first login, I'd recommend immediately going to your User
> Settings to change your password and set up 2FA if it's not already required."

### THE MEMBER DASHBOARD (2:30 – 4:00)

**[SCREEN: The main Dashboard loads. Show it from a member's perspective —
fewer widgets than the Chief's view but still informative.]**

> "Welcome to your dashboard. This is your home screen. Let's see what's here."

**[SCREEN: Point to each widget]**

> "**Upcoming Events** — the next few events you need to know about. You'll see
> the event name, date, time, and your RSVP status. Green check means you've
> RSVPed yes. If there's no status, you haven't responded yet."

> "**My Shifts** — your upcoming shift assignments. If scheduling is enabled,
> you'll see when you're on duty."

> "**Training Status** — a quick view of your certification health. Green means
> everything's current. Yellow means something's expiring soon. Red means
> something's expired."

> "**Notifications** — any messages, reminders, or action items that need your
> attention."

**[CALLOUT: "Your dashboard = your personal fire department briefing"]**

**[TRANSITION: Events section]**

---

## CHAPTER 3: Events & RSVPs (4:00 – 8:00)

### VIEWING EVENTS (4:00 – 5:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Events (EventsPage)]**

> "Click Events in the sidebar to see all upcoming events. You'll see a list or
> calendar view of everything scheduled — drill nights, business meetings,
> training sessions, social events, fundraisers."

**[SCREEN: Show the events list with event cards]**

> "Each event card shows the name, date, time, location, and how many people
> have RSVPed. Click on any event to see the full details."

### RSVPING TO AN EVENT (5:00 – 6:00)

**[SCREEN: Click into an event detail page (EventDetailPage)]**

> "Open an event and you'll see the full description, location, and who's
> attending. To RSVP, click the RSVP button."

**[SCREEN: Show the RSVP options — Yes, No, Maybe]**

> "Choose Yes, No, or Maybe. If you choose No, you can optionally add a reason.
> Your RSVP can be changed anytime before the RSVP deadline."

**[SCREEN: Click "Yes" and show the confirmation]**

> "That's it. Your officers can now see you're coming. Done in five seconds."

**[CALLOUT: "RSVP in 5 seconds — no more group text chains"]**

### QR CODE CHECK-IN (6:00 – 7:00)

**[SCREEN: Show the Event Self Check-In page (EventSelfCheckInPage)]**

> "When you arrive at an event, if QR check-in is enabled, you'll see a QR code
> posted at the venue — usually on a tablet or printed sheet at the door."

**[SCREEN: Show a QR code displayed on screen]**

> "Open your phone's camera or The Logbook app, scan the QR code, and you're
> checked in. No paper sign-in sheet, no waiting in line."

> "If you don't have your phone, a Captain or officer with manage access can
> manually check you in."

### PAST EVENTS & YOUR HISTORY (7:00 – 8:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Past Events tab (PastEventsTab)]**

> "Click the Past Events tab to see your attendance history. You can see every
> event you've attended, missed, or been excused from."

**[SCREEN: Show past events list with attendance markers]**

> "This is the same data your officers see when they review your participation.
> If you're going for a leadership position or applying for a certification that
> requires minimum attendance, this is your documentation."

**[TRANSITION: Training section]**

---

## CHAPTER 4: Your Training & Certifications (8:00 – 12:00)

### MY TRAINING PAGE (8:00 – 9:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to My Training (MyTrainingPage)]**

> "Click Training in the sidebar, then My Training. This is your personal
> training record — everything you've completed, everything that's current,
> and everything that's expiring."

**[SCREEN: Show the My Training page with certification cards/list]**

> "Each certification shows the name, your completion date, the expiration date,
> and a status indicator. Green means current. Yellow means expiring within 90
> days. Red means expired."

**[CALLOUT: Color-coded status: "Green = current. Yellow = expiring soon. Red = expired."]**

> "Click on any certification to see the details — when you completed it, who
> recorded it, and any attached documentation like scanned certificates."

### SUBMITTING EXTERNAL TRAINING (9:30 – 11:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Submit Training (SubmitTrainingPage)]**

> "Did you take a class on your own — a CPR refresher through the Red Cross, a
> Hazmat course at the fire academy? You can submit it for credit."

**[SCREEN: Click "Submit Training"]**

> "Select the training requirement it fulfills — 'CPR/AED Certification.'
> Enter the completion date and expiration date if applicable."

**[SCREEN: Fill in the form]**

> "**Upload your certificate.** This is the proof your Training Officer needs
> to approve the submission. Take a photo of the paper cert or upload the PDF
> if you received it digitally."

**[SCREEN: Upload a file]**

> "Add any notes — the name of the course, the instructor, the institution.
> Then submit."

**[SCREEN: Submit the form. Show the success message.]**

> "Your submission goes to the Training Officer for review. You'll get a
> notification when it's approved or if they need more information."

**[CALLOUT: "Always upload your certificate — it speeds up approval"]**

### TRAINING PROGRAMS & COURSE LIBRARY (11:00 – 12:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Course Library (CourseLibraryPage)]**

> "The Course Library shows all available training programs and their
> requirements. This is your roadmap — what do you need to complete to stay
> compliant?"

**[SCREEN: Show the course library with programs and their requirements]**

> "Click on a program to see each requirement and your status. If you're working
> toward a specific certification, this tells you exactly what's left."

**[TRANSITION: Scheduling]**

---

## CHAPTER 5: Scheduling & Shift Swaps (12:00 – 15:00)

### VIEWING YOUR SCHEDULE (12:00 – 13:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Scheduling (SchedulingPage)]**

> "The Scheduling page shows the department's shift calendar. You can see your
> assignments highlighted — which shifts you're on, which positions you're
> filling."

**[SCREEN: Show the calendar with shifts highlighted]**

> "Toggle between week and month view. Click on any shift to see who else is
> assigned and what the coverage looks like."

### REQUESTING A SHIFT SWAP (13:00 – 14:30)

> "Need to swap a shift? Maybe you have a family commitment or a schedule
> conflict. The Logbook handles this digitally — no more calling around to find
> someone to cover."

**[SCREEN: Click on one of your assigned shifts. Click "Request Swap."]**

> "Click on the shift you need to swap, then click 'Request Swap.' You can
> either propose a specific swap — 'I'll trade Tuesday for your Thursday' — or
> just post the shift as available for anyone to pick up."

**[SCREEN: Show the swap request form with options]**

> "If you propose a specific swap with another member, they'll get a
> notification to accept or decline. Once both parties agree, it goes to the
> officer for final approval."

> "If you post it as open, any eligible member can claim it."

**[SCREEN: Submit the swap request. Show the pending status.]**

> "You'll be notified when the swap is approved, declined, or claimed."

### MY EQUIPMENT (14:30 – 15:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to My Equipment (MyEquipmentPage)]**

> "While we're talking about your assignments, the My Equipment page shows any
> gear assigned to you — radios, pagers, turnout gear, specialized equipment.
> If your department tracks equipment assignments, you can see exactly what's
> checked out to you."

**[TRANSITION: Documents and directory]**

---

## CHAPTER 6: Documents, Directory & Elections (15:00 – 18:00)

### DOCUMENTS & SOPs (15:00 – 15:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Documents (DocumentsPage)]**

> "The Documents module is your digital filing cabinet. SOPs, policies,
> training materials, forms — everything the department publishes lives here.
> Browse by folder, search by keyword, and download what you need."

**[SCREEN: Browse folders, search for a document, open and download one]**

### MEMBER DIRECTORY (15:30 – 16:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Members list]**

> "The member directory shows everyone in the department. You can see names,
> positions, station assignments, and — depending on your permissions — contact
> information. This is your department phonebook."

**[SCREEN: Show the directory with search and filtering]**

### VOTING IN ELECTIONS (16:00 – 17:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Elections. Show an active election.]**

> "When an election is active, you'll see a notification on your dashboard.
> Click into Elections to see the ballot."

**[SCREEN: Open the ballot voting page (BallotVotingPage)]**

> "The ballot shows each office being contested and the candidates running.
> Read their statements, make your selections, and cast your vote."

**[SCREEN: Walk through selecting candidates and submitting the ballot]**

> "Your vote is confidential — only the aggregate results are visible to
> officers. You can only vote once per election, so make sure you're done
> before submitting."

**[CALLOUT: "Your vote is confidential — only aggregate results are published"]**

### MEETING MINUTES (17:00 – 17:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Minutes (MinutesPage)]**

> "Published meeting minutes are accessible to all members. Search by date or
> keyword to find what was discussed at any past meeting."

**[SCREEN: Show browsing and searching minutes]**

### NOTIFICATIONS (17:30 – 18:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to Notifications (NotificationsPage)]**

> "The Notifications page shows everything that's been sent to you — event
> reminders, training expirations, shift changes, announcements. You can manage
> your notification preferences here too — choose what you want email alerts
> for versus what you only want to see in-app."

**[SCREEN: Show notification preferences]**

**[TRANSITION: Profile and wrap-up]**

---

## CHAPTER 7: Your Profile & Personal Settings (18:00 – 20:00)

### USER SETTINGS (18:00 – 19:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to User Settings (UserSettingsPage)]**

> "Your personal settings are where you customize the platform for yourself."

**[SCREEN: Walk through settings options]**

> "**Profile:** Update your name, phone number, emergency contact, and profile
> photo."

> "**Security:** Change your password, set up or manage two-factor
> authentication."

> "**Preferences:** Choose your theme — light mode, dark mode, or match your
> system. Set your notification preferences."

**[SCREEN: Show toggling dark mode on. The interface changes.]**

> "Dark mode is nice for using The Logbook during night shifts or in low-light
> environments."

### YOUR MEMBER PROFILE (19:00 – 19:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to your own Member Profile (MemberProfilePage)]**

> "Your member profile is what other members see when they look you up. It shows
> your name, position, station, and attendance and training stats. Make sure
> your profile photo is uploaded — it appears on your ID card and in the
> directory."

### MOBILE ACCESS (19:30 – 20:00)

> "The Logbook is a Progressive Web App — which means it works on your phone's
> browser just like a native app. On most phones, you can 'Add to Home Screen'
> to get an app icon."

**[SCREEN: Show adding The Logbook to the home screen on a mobile device.
Show the app opening full-screen like a native app.]**

> "You get push notifications, offline access to recently viewed content, and
> the full interface optimized for your screen size. It's the same platform,
> same data, just on your phone."

**[CALLOUT: "Add to Home Screen = full app experience on your phone"]**

### WRAP-UP (20:00 – 20:30)

> "That's everything you need as a member. RSVP to events, check your training,
> view the schedule, swap shifts, vote in elections, and access documents — all
> from one login."

> "If your department hasn't started using The Logbook yet, share this video
> with your officers. And if you're already on it, go RSVP for that next drill."

**[SCREEN: End card with subscribe, next video link, and playlist link]**
