## CHAPTER 1: What a Training Pipeline Is (0:00 – 2:30)

### HOOK (0:00 – 0:30)

**[SCREEN: A program detail page showing a phased "Firefighter Recruit School"
with a phase timeline, an enrolled-members list, and progress bars.]**

> "Certifications tell you *what* a member has. A pipeline tells you *where they
> are on the way there*. If you run a recruit school, a driver-operator program,
> or any multi-step path where people earn their way forward, this is the tool
> that tracks every one of them — automatically — from day one to certified."

### PROGRAM vs. REQUIREMENT vs. PIPELINE (0:30 – 1:30)

> "Quick vocabulary, because these three get mixed up. A **requirement** is one
> thing a member has to do — forty hours of fireground skills, a hazmat
> certification, a passed pump test. A **program**, or pipeline, is an ordered
> collection of those requirements — recruit school is a program made of many
> requirements. And an **enrollment** is one member's journey through that
> program."

**[CALLOUT: "Requirement = one task · Pipeline = the whole path · Enrollment = one member on it"]**

### WHY IT BEATS A SPREADSHEET (1:30 – 2:30)

> "Here's what makes it worth the setup. Once a member is enrolled, their
> progress updates *on its own* — from the shifts they work, the sessions you
> approve, the skills they pass, the courses they import. You're not
> transcribing anything. Your job shifts from data entry to actually watching who
> needs help. And the system tells you who that is."

## CHAPTER 2: Enrolling Members (2:30 – 5:30)

### THE ENROLLMENTS TAB (2:30 – 3:15)

**[SCREEN: Open a program → the Enrollments tab, showing enrolled members by
name with progress percentages.]**

> "Every pipeline has an **Enrollments** tab. This is your roster — each enrolled
> member by name, their current phase, and their overall percentage. No user IDs,
> no guessing. To add someone, click **Enroll**."

### THE ELIGIBILITY-AWARE PICKER (3:15 – 4:30)

**[SCREEN: The enroll picker with a searchable member list, each tagged
Eligible / Enrolled / Prerequisite / In another program.]**

> "The enroll picker is smart. It pre-checks who can actually be enrolled and
> defaults to **Show eligible only**. Each member is tagged: **Eligible** and
> ready, already **Enrolled** here, blocked by a **Prerequisite** they haven't
> finished, or a soft note that they're **also in another program**."

**[CALLOUT: "Hard gates: already enrolled · unmet prerequisite"]**

> "Two things are hard blocks — being already enrolled in *this* program, and
> missing a prerequisite program you set. Being active in *another* program is
> only an advisory. A new recruit can absolutely be in several onboarding paths
> at once, so those members stay selectable — they're just flagged so you know."

### BULK ENROLL (4:30 – 5:30)

**[SCREEN: Select multiple eligible members, click Enroll, show the confirmation.]**

> "Enrolling a whole recruit class? Select them all and enroll in one action.
> Anyone blocked by a real gate — a missing prerequisite — is held back and
> reported, so you never get a silent half-enrollment. Each member starts at the
> first phase with a fresh progress record."

## CHAPTER 3: How Progress Updates — The Feeds (5:30 – 9:30)

### THE BIG IDEA (5:30 – 6:15)

**[SCREEN: A single requirement's progress bar ticking up, with small labels:
"shift report", "session", "skills test", "import".]**

> "This is the heart of it. A requirement's progress can advance from six
> different places, and they all feed the same progress bar. Let's walk them."

### THE SIX FEEDS (6:15 – 8:00)

**[CALLOUT: The six feeds appearing one at a time]**

> "**One — approved training sessions.** When you approve a session that's linked
> to a program, every attendee's linked requirement gets the hours."

> "**Two — shift completion reports.** File a shift report and the trainee's
> shift, call, and hour requirements advance automatically."

> "**Three — skills tests.** Pass a skills test that's linked to a requirement,
> and that requirement is complete."

> "**Four — external course imports.** A course synced from a provider like
> Vector Solutions credits any requirement it matches by category — if you've
> allowed external credit on that requirement."

> "**Five — applying a self-report.** When you approve a member's self-reported
> training, you can apply it straight to a pipeline requirement — perfect for
> make-up sessions."

> "**Six — your manual entry.** And of course you can always log hours or mark a
> requirement complete by hand from a member's progress detail."

### TWO GUARANTEES (8:00 – 9:30)

**[SCREEN: A member's progress detail; hover on a requirement to show it's
officer-verified.]**

> "Two things protect the integrity of all this. First — **only officers can
> complete or credit a requirement.** A member can mark something *in progress*,
> but setting hours, recording a score, or marking a requirement done is on you.
> Members earn credit by submitting for review, not by editing their own bar to
> a hundred percent."

**[CALLOUT: "No double-counting — one training, counted once"]**

> "Second — **no double-counting.** Every one of those feeds remembers the exact
> source it credited. If the same shift report gets re-filed, the same course
> re-syncs, or the same session is re-finalized, the system recognizes it and
> doesn't add the hours twice. One real training counts once — no matter how many
> times the paperwork moves."

## CHAPTER 4: Phases & Advancement (9:30 – 12:00)

### HOW PHASES WORK (9:30 – 10:30)

**[SCREEN: A phased program's timeline — Orientation & Safety → Fireground Skills
→ Fire Attack & Live-Fire → Certification — with a member marked on phase 2.]**

> "Most real programs are phased. A recruit finishes Orientation before touching
> live fire. When every *required* requirement in a member's current phase is
> done, the system **auto-advances** them to the next phase — and rolls their
> overall percentage forward."

### MANUAL-ADVANCE GATES (10:30 – 11:15)

**[SCREEN: The phase editor showing a "require officer approval to advance"
toggle on the Certification phase.]**

> "But some phases you don't want to hand off automatically — the safety phase,
> the certification phase. Flag those with **require officer approval to
> advance**, and auto-advance stops there. The member waits on the doorstep until
> *you* move them forward — a deliberate checkpoint for the moments that matter."

### ADVANCING A MEMBER (11:15 – 12:00)

**[SCREEN: On a member's progress, click "Advance" on a gated phase; show the
optional force override.]**

> "When they're ready, advance them with a click. And if you ever need to move
> someone forward before every box is checked — a documented exception — there's
> a **force** override. Use it sparingly, but it's there when reality doesn't fit
> the checklist."

## CHAPTER 5: Recertification Cycles (12:00 – 14:30)

### THE PROBLEM (12:00 – 12:45)

> "Some certifications expire. NREMT recertifies every two years. When that clock
> resets, a member's accumulated progress needs to clear so a fresh cycle can
> start — but you don't want to rebuild the pipeline every two years. The recert
> cycle handles it."

### AUTOMATIC RESET (12:45 – 13:45)

**[SCREEN: The pipeline's Edit dialog → enable "Recertification cycle", set a
cycle length in months, optionally a calendar anchor.]**

> "In the pipeline's Edit dialog, turn on **Recertification cycle** and set a
> length — twenty-four months for NREMT. Optionally pin a calendar anchor, like
> March 30th, so every cycle lands on the same date; leave it blank to roll
> forward from each member's own enrollment date. Each enrollment then tracks its
> next reset. When that date passes, the member is reset for a new cycle and the
> deadline advances automatically."

**[CALLOUT: "Daily 5 AM sweep resets everyone who's due — even the ones nobody's watching"]**

> "A daily sweep at 5 AM resets every past-due enrollment across the department,
> so members no one is actively watching still start their new cycle on time."

### MANUAL RESET (13:45 – 14:30)

**[SCREEN: A member's progress modal → "Reset" on one requirement, and "Start
new cycle" for the whole enrollment.]**

> "You can also reset by hand. In a member's progress modal, **Reset** clears one
> requirement; **Start new cycle** resets everything and returns them to phase
> one. Both ask you to confirm first. Use it when a cert is close to or past
> expiry and you want to restart that member right now."

> "And here's a nice detail — when a cycle resets, the pace clock resets with it.
> A member fresh into a new cycle isn't flagged 'behind' on day one just because
> the calendar rolled over."

## CHAPTER 6: Safeguards & Fixing Mistakes (14:30 – 18:00)

### THE SAFEGUARDS, RECAPPED (14:30 – 15:30)

**[CALLOUT: Three shields appearing — "Officer-only completion", "No
self-approval", "No accidental resurrection"]**

> "A few protections run quietly in the background so the numbers stay honest.
> Members can't complete their own requirements. An officer can't approve their
> *own* self-reported training — a second officer has to sign it off, so nobody
> grants themselves hours. And a member who's been withdrawn or failed isn't
> silently flipped back to 'completed' if some late credit pushes them to a
> hundred percent."

### VOIDING A RECORD (15:30 – 16:30)

**[SCREEN: A member's training history → the "Void" action on a record entered
in error.]**

> "Now the part everyone eventually needs: undoing a mistake. Say a record was
> entered wrong, or twice. **Void** it. The record is marked cancelled — kept for
> your audit trail, never truly deleted — and any pipeline credit it fed comes
> right back off. Because compliance only counts completed records, a voided one
> stops counting immediately. You don't touch anyone's progress by hand."

### REVERSING AN APPROVAL (16:30 – 17:30)

**[SCREEN: An already-approved submission → the "Reverse Approval" action.]**

> "Approved the wrong submission? On an approved submission, **Reverse Approval**
> unwinds the whole thing — it voids the record that approval created, takes back
> the pipeline credit it applied, and sends the submission back to **pending
> review** so you can re-decide. Reject it, or re-approve it with corrected hours.
> Either way, the requirement math fixes itself."

### WHY THIS MATTERS (17:30 – 18:00)

**[CALLOUT: "Every fix is one click and fully audited"]**

> "The whole point: mistakes shouldn't be scary. Every correction is a single
> action, it unwinds progress automatically, and it's written to the audit log.
> You can always make it right."

## CHAPTER 7: Staying Ahead of Trouble — Alerts (18:00 – 20:00)

### FALLING-BEHIND ALERTS (18:00 – 19:00)

**[SCREEN: A notification: "Recruit Jane Doe is falling behind in Recruit School
— 20% complete, deadline in 5 days".]**

> "You can't watch every recruit every day, so the system watches for you. A
> weekly check flags anyone who's behind pace or approaching a deadline, and it
> notifies **both the member and you** — the training officers — so someone can
> step in before it's a crisis."

**[CALLOUT: "Alerts go to the member AND their officers"]**

### SMART, NOT NOISY (19:00 – 20:00)

> "And it's tuned to not become noise. A member who stays behind isn't re-alerted
> every single week — there's a cooldown. Pace is measured from their current
> cycle, so someone who just restarted a recert cycle isn't wrongly flagged.
> Deadline warnings fire at thirty, fourteen, and seven days out. You get the
> signal that matters, when it matters."

## CHAPTER 8: Your Pipeline Routine & Wrap-Up (20:00 – 22:00)

### THE WEEKLY RHYTHM (20:00 – 21:15)

**[CALLOUT: Routine steps appearing one at a time]**

> "Here's a rhythm that keeps a pipeline healthy."

> "**Weekly:** Open the Enrollments tab and scan for anyone stalled. Clear your
> pending self-report reviews and shift reports — those are the feeds that move
> everyone's progress. Act on any falling-behind alerts."

> "**As sessions happen:** Approve linked training sessions and skills tests
> promptly — every day you sit on an approval is a day the recruit's bar doesn't
> move."

> "**At each gate:** When a member reaches a manual-advance phase, review and
> advance them. That's your quality checkpoint."

> "**Each cycle:** Let the automatic recert reset do its job, and spot-check that
> members starting a new cycle look right."

### WRAP-UP (21:15 – 22:00)

> "A pipeline turns a messy, multi-month process into something you can see at a
> glance and correct in a click. Set it up once, let the feeds do the tracking,
> and spend your time on the members who actually need you."

> "If you haven't built one yet, watch the setup video next — I'll take you
> through building a pipeline from a template, step by step. And to see what all
> this looks like from a recruit's side, check out the member's pipeline guide."
