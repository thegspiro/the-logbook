# Script 11: Building a Training Pipeline — Step-by-Step Setup

**Video Type:** Focused Tutorial (Short/Medium-Form)
**Estimated Length:** 12–16 minutes
**Target Audience:** Training Officers setting up a pipeline for the first time
**Roles Covered:** training_officer, safety_officer
**Chapters:** 8 (each designed as a standalone clip)
**Requires permission:** `training.manage`

> **Companion videos:** Script 9 is the officer's operating manual for a *running*
> pipeline; Script 10 shows the member's side. This video is purely about
> **building** one — from an empty screen to an enrolled recruit class.

## CHAPTER 2: Creating the Program (1:30 – 3:00)

### THE CREATE-PIPELINE WIZARD (1:30 – 2:15)

**[SCREEN: Click "Create Pipeline" → the wizard opens on the program-details
step.]**

> "Click **Create Pipeline**. This wizard builds the whole thing in one shot — so
> if anything goes wrong partway, you don't get a half-built mess left behind.
> First, the basics: a **name** — 'Firefighter Recruit School' — a description, a
> **code** and **version** if your department tracks those, and an overall **time
> limit** if the program has a hard deadline."

### CHOOSING A STRUCTURE (2:15 – 3:00)

**[SCREEN: The structure-type selector — Sequential / Phases / Flexible.]**

> "Then pick a **structure**. **Sequential** is a straight ordered list.
> **Phases** groups requirements into stages that unlock in order — this is what
> most recruit schools want. **Flexible** lets members work requirements in any
> order. We'll choose **Phases**."

**[CALLOUT: "Phases = stages that unlock in order (best for recruit schools)"]**

**[TRANSITION: Adding phases]**

## CHAPTER 3: Building the Phases (3:00 – 4:30)

### ADDING PHASES (3:00 – 4:00)

**[SCREEN: Add phases one at a time — Orientation & Safety, Fireground Skills,
Fire Attack & Live-Fire, Certification.]**

> "Now lay out the journey. Add your phases in order — **Orientation & Safety**,
> **Fireground Skills**, **Fire Attack & Live-Fire**, **Certification**. Give
> each a name, an optional description, and an optional per-phase time limit.
> Drag to reorder any time."

### THE MANUAL-ADVANCE FLAG (4:00 – 4:30)

**[SCREEN: Toggle "require officer approval to advance" on the Safety and
Certification phases.]**

> "Here's the important toggle: **require officer approval to advance**. Turn it
> on for phases where a human must sign off — safety, certification. With it on,
> members can't auto-advance past that phase; an officer moves them. Leave it off
> and they flow forward automatically as they finish."

**[TRANSITION: Adding requirements]**

## CHAPTER 4: Adding Requirements (4:30 – 8:00)

### THE REQUIREMENT TYPES (4:30 – 6:00)

**[SCREEN: Add a requirement to phase 2 → the requirement-type dropdown showing
all eight types.]**

> "Inside each phase, add the **requirements** — the actual things a recruit has
> to do. Pick a type, and the type decides how progress is measured:"

**[CALLOUT: Types appearing one at a time]**

> "**Hours** — accumulate training hours. **Courses** — complete a number of
> named courses. **Certification** — hold a specific cert. **Shifts** and
> **Calls** — count shifts worked or calls run. **Skills evaluation** — pass a
> hands-on skill. **Checklist** — a set of items signed off. And **Knowledge
> test** — a scored test with a passing threshold."

> "For an hours requirement you'll set the **target hours**; for shifts or calls,
> the count; for a knowledge test, the **passing score** — default seventy
> percent — and how many attempts are allowed."

### REQUIRED vs. OPTIONAL (6:00 – 6:30)

**[SCREEN: The Required ↔ Optional toggle on a requirement.]**

> "Each requirement has a **Required or Optional** toggle. Only *required*
> requirements count toward advancing a phase and toward the overall percentage.
> Optional ones are there for enrichment without blocking progress."

### LINKING REQUIREMENTS SO THEY AUTO-UPDATE (6:30 – 8:00)

**[SCREEN: On an hours requirement, link it to a training category; show the
"allow external credit" toggle.]**

> "This next part is what makes a pipeline feel magic instead of manual: **link
> your requirements** so the feeds can fill them in. Tie an hours requirement to
> a **training category**, and any approved session or shift in that category
> credits it automatically. Tie a skills requirement to a **skills evaluation**,
> and passing the test completes it."

**[CALLOUT: "Link requirements → progress fills in automatically"]**

> "Two toggles worth calling out here. **Allow external credit** — turn this on if
> a course imported from an outside provider should be able to satisfy this
> requirement. Leave it off for requirements that must be delivered in-house."

**[SCREEN: On the Create Session form, show the "Counts toward certification
requirements" toggle.]**

> "And on the session side there's **'Counts toward certification requirements.'**
> Leave it on for real, certification-grade delivery. Turn it off for an informal
> drill — attendees still get the hours and the record, but it won't feed the
> certificate requirement. That keeps ineligible hours off a member's cert
> progress."

**[TRANSITION: Milestones]**

## CHAPTER 5: Milestones (8:00 – 9:00)

**[SCREEN: Add milestones at 25%, 50%, 75% completion.]**

> "**Milestones** are checkpoints that fire a notification when a member hits
> them — twenty-five, fifty, seventy-five percent, or whatever markers matter to
> you. They're great for morale and for keeping officers in the loop as a recruit
> class progresses. Add them, name them, and optionally require officer
> verification. They're purely additive — they don't block anything."

**[TRANSITION: Recertification]**

---

## CHAPTER 6: Recertification Cycle (9:00 – 10:30)

**[SCREEN: The pipeline's Edit dialog → enable "Recertification cycle", set 24
months, optionally a calendar anchor.]**

> "If this pipeline tracks something that **expires** — an NREMT level, an annual
> compliance set — set up the **Recertification cycle** in the Edit dialog. Turn
> it on and set a length in months: twenty-four for NREMT's two-year cycle."

**[CALLOUT: "Calendar anchor = every cycle lands on the same date · blank = rolls from enrollment"]**

> "Optionally pin a **calendar anchor** — a fixed reset month and day — so every
> member's cycle lands on the same date. Leave it blank and each member's cycle
> rolls forward from their own enrollment date. Once it's on, the system resets
> members automatically when their cycle is due — including a daily sweep that
> catches anyone past due. You build it once; it repeats forever."

**[TRANSITION: Prerequisites and enrollment rules]**

---

## CHAPTER 7: Prerequisites & Enrollment Rules (10:30 – 11:45)

**[SCREEN: Program settings → set a prerequisite program and the concurrent-
enrollment option.]**

> "Two rules shape who can enroll. Set a **prerequisite program** — say, they
> must finish 'Recruit School' before 'Driver Candidate' — and the system blocks
> enrollment until it's met. That's a hard gate."

> "Then the **concurrent enrollment** option. If you want members in only one
> program at a time, set it — but know it's a *soft advisory*, not a hard block.
> A member can absolutely be in several programs at once, which is normal for a
> new recruit juggling onboarding paths. The picker just flags it so you're
> aware."

**[CALLOUT: "Prerequisite = hard block · Concurrent = soft advisory"]**

**[TRANSITION: Publish and enroll]**

## CHAPTER 8: Publish, Enroll, and You're Live (11:45 – 14:00)

### SAVING THE BUILD (11:45 – 12:15)

**[SCREEN: Click through the final wizard step; the program is created and its
detail page opens.]**

> "Finish the wizard and it builds everything — phases, requirements, milestones
> — in a single step, and drops you on the program's detail page. Nothing's
> half-done. If you ever need to change something later, this whole page is
> editable — add a phase, move a requirement, adjust a target — and it re-computes
> enrolled members' progress for you."

### ENROLLING YOUR FIRST CLASS (12:15 – 13:15)

**[SCREEN: The Enrollments tab → Enroll → the eligibility-aware member picker →
select the recruit class → Enroll.]**

> "Now bring in people. Open the **Enrollments** tab, click **Enroll**, and use
> the member picker. It pre-checks eligibility and defaults to showing only
> eligible members, tagging anyone blocked by a prerequisite. Select your recruit
> class, enroll them all in one action, and every one of them starts at phase one
> with a fresh progress record."

### YOU'RE LIVE (13:15 – 14:00)

**[SCREEN: The Enrollments tab now populated with recruits at 0%, ready to
climb.]**

> "That's it — you're live. From here, the feeds take over: approve their
> sessions and skills tests, file their shift reports, and watch the bars climb
> on their own. To run the pipeline day to day — advancing members, fixing
> mistakes, reading the alerts — watch the officer's pipeline guide next."

**[SCREEN: End card with subscribe, next-video link (Script 9), and playlist link]**
